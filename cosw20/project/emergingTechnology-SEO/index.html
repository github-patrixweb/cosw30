SEO - What is It?

search en·gine op·ti·mi·za·tion

Defined by: a dictionary entry on a Google search results page (May 14, 2018)

I've been publishing websites for clients twenty-three years now, and a common client concern is they want their website ranking in the top position of organic search results. What are organic search results? They are the natural results opposed to un-natural pay-per-click results. My personal experience has been clicking non-organic links take me to a website not related to what I'm searching for, so I usually start at the top of the helpful organic results. That is usually below the links that say advertisement, and sometimes so many ads display, I must scroll down to see the organic results. Because most users avoid non-organic search results, it is in your best interest to learn search engine optimization. Design your websites using SEO, and keep current with the changing algorithms.

Some basic DIY SEO techniques you can use are:

  • keywords used in pages you hope will be searched for (but not over-used)
  • quality - pages include content that is well written, current - update content regularly and link to and from quality sources (social media links really help if they are from reputable sources)
  • content that changes to answers via search
  • include images, video and news
  • make your site responsive - in 2016 Google updated their search engines to filter for mobile friendly sites
  • streamline your site for fast loads - remember dial-up connections are still in use
  • include meaningful keywords for topics in the URL
  • use HTTPS
  • unique titles for every page that include keywords matching the content of the page
  • use headings and sub-headings with relevant keywords identifying the content
  • description: a meta tag describing the content of the page. Remember not to exceed 160 characters.

Above are a dozen tips for SEO applied to your website. Remember, use them ethically. Improper use can negatively affect your website, and can result in lower ranking or blacklist from the results. If you have the means, hire an ethical SEO professional and achieve superior results.

 

Pros and Cons of SEO

 
 

Pros

  1. Good SEO increases traffic to your website.
  2. Higher traffic increases potential sales/profit.
  3. More revenue increases your web budget for system improvements.

Cons

  1. Bad SEO decreases traffic from your website.
  2. Lower traffic decreases potential sales/profit.
  3. Less revenue decreases your web budget for system improvements.
